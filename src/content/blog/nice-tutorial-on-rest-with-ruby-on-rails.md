Now a days everybody is talking about **REST**. Here I does not mean by taking rest after some work; it is about **Representational State Transfer**. After searching a lot about REST and reading many artciles I was still unable to completely understand actually what is REST? Today I was searching on internet for something realted to Ruby on Rails and accidently I have found an article (later I came to know that it is a series of articles) about implementing REST with Ruby on Rails on [Softies on Rails](http://softiesonrails.com/) . This website is to help the .NET programmers who want to learn Ruby on Rails. I have liked the articles a lot. Now I am not so confused about REST as I was earlier. The articles are written in such a easy language that a very new programmer can also understand. Although the articles are specially related to Rails but anyone can take benefit of them.

The tutorial is in 5 parts, the links are as following –

1\. [Part 1 – Understanding Resource](http://softiesonrails.com/2007/3/28/rest-101-part-1-understanding-resources)
2\. [Part 2 – A Million APIs](http://softiesonrails.com/2007/4/3/rest-101-part-2-a-million-apis)
3\. [Part 3 – RESTful Design](http://softiesonrails.com/2007/4/10/rest-101-part-3-just-call-me-the-repo-man)
4\. [Part 4 – Routing](http://softiesonrails.com/2007/4/18/rest-101-part-4-routing)
5\. [Part 5 – Respond!](http://softiesonrails.com/2007/5/1/rest-101-part-5-respond)

I will recommend these articles to anyone who wants to know about REST.
